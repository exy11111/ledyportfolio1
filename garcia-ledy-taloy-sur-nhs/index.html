ACKNOWLEDGEMENTS

I express my profound gratitude to my beloved school, Benguet State University. This institution has become a second home and a nurturing ground for my growth and development as an aspiring educator. I will forever cherish the values, knowledge, and experiences I have gained throughout my stay.

To Ma’am Divina Yango, the esteemed Dean of the College of Teacher Education, thank you for your leadership and for upholding the excellence of our college. Your presence has always been an inspiration to us students. To all the College of Teacher Education instructors, thank you for sharing your wisdom and shaping us not only as future educators but also as compassionate individuals. Your impact on our lives goes far beyond the classroom. A heartfelt special mention goes to Ma'am Dolores Alawas, the Department Chair of Bachelor of Secondary Education, and he ever-passionate adviser of the Association of Language and Literature Students (ALLS), for your motherly mentorship, for believing in us, and for continually inspiring us to serve and lead with heart and purpose. I am also deeply thankful to Ma’am Alma Gallardo, our Supervising Instructor, whose unwavering support, encouragement, and constructive feedback guided us during one of the most pivotal moments of our academic life.

To BSU-Secondary Laboratory School, thank you for being the first institution to welcome and train me as a future educator. I will always be grateful for the lessons and experiences I gained during my stay. To the teachers at SLS, thank you for warmly welcoming us into your school community and the opportunities you have given us to grow. My special thanks to Sir Agustin Nang-is, my first-ever Cooperating Teacher, for his patience, trust, and guidance. You have greatly influenced how I see my role as a teacher, and I will always be grateful for your support.

To Taloy Sur National High School, headed by Sir Gabriel L. Balangay, thank you so much for accommodating us. This school holds a special place in my heart, as it allowed me to experience what it truly means to be a teacher, not just within the four corners of the classroom, but also through the many duties and responsibilities that come with the profession. My deepest gratitude goes to Ma'am Euresa W. Laruan, my ever-supportive Cooperating Teacher. Thank you for your unwavering guidance, for the wise pieces of advice you generously shared, and for always helping me craft and improve my lesson plans. Your patience and encouragement made a big difference in my journey.

To my co-interns at Taloy, thank you for showing me the true meaning of unity and leadership. Working with you all was an unforgettable experience, and I am proud to have grown alongside such dedicated and passionate individuals. To my students in Grade 7 - A and B, thank you for being part of my journey. You have truly tested my patience, but in doing so, you also helped me grow. You made me realize so many things about myself, about teaching, and the beauty of learning. I will always carry the lessons I learned from you in my heart.

To my family, Mama, Myko, and Mick, the very foundation of my strength, thank you for standing beside me in every challenge I encountered. Your emotional, spiritual, and financial support sustained me through every sleepless night and every doubt I had to overcome. Thank you for believing in my dreams even when I found it hard to believe in myself. To my relatives and friends, thank you for being my constant source of joy, encouragement, and companionship.

Thank you, Exequiel, for being the best listener to my rants, the cheerleader to my quiet victories, and the hand that helped me hold on through all the chaos. To my dearest friends, thank you for the endless chats, the laughter, the tears, and the reminders to never give up.

Above all, I give my deepest praise and thanksgiving to Almighty God, who has carried me through every trial and triumph. Thank you for providing me with the strength to persevere, for comforting me in my silent battles, and for making a way even when there seemed to be none. Your grace has sustained me throughout my college life, especially in this challenging but fulfilling final year.
