CHALLENGES

Throughout my internship, I encountered a range of challenges that tested both my professional and personal limits. Classroom management was one of the major difficulties—my students were often noisy, making it hard to maintain order and deliver lessons effectively. This was especially challenging during assessments and discussions. Another pressing issue was the lack of materials, particularly when preparing for our February extension activity. We wanted to create a meaningful and memorable experience for our students, but the school had very limited supplies. On a personal note, I struggled emotionally. I experienced episodes of emotional breakdowns—symptoms of my long-standing battle with depression, which made everyday tasks feel heavier than they should have been. Adding to these difficulties was the leaking roof of the teachers' quarters. Every time it rained, water would seep through, risking our belongings and disrupting our rest.

INTERVENTION

To address the classroom behavior issues, I implemented a behavioral chart system. This served as a visual guide to promote responsibility and accountability among students, and it helped create a more structured environment. When it came to our extension activity, my co-interns and I chose to contribute our own money to buy materials. Though it was a financial strain, it was a sacrifice we were willing to make to give the students a fun and creative experience. To cope with my emotional struggles, I relied on healthy coping mechanisms: I talked to people I trusted, spent weekends at home to refresh my mind, prayed, and poured my emotions into journaling. These helped me gain strength and keep going, even when it felt overwhelming. As for the leaking quarters, we used pails to catch the dripping water and moved our valuables to safer spots. We reported the issue to the principal, but instead of repairs, we were advised to sleep in the clinic, which we eventually considered just to avoid further inconvenience.

THE RESULT GENERATED

These interventions brought positive outcomes. The behavioral chart proved to be effective—students began to show more interest and self-regulation, making the classroom atmosphere more conducive to learning. Our extension activity was a big success; despite the limited resources, the students appreciated and enjoyed every bit of it. Emotionally, I didn’t just survive—I grew stronger. The process of unwinding, praying, and journaling helped me find balance and heal, even when things felt heavy. Lastly, while the leaking roof was never fixed, we managed to keep our things dry and adapt to the situation with resilience. These challenges reminded me that teaching goes beyond delivering lessons—it’s about patience, creativity, empathy, and the willingness to keep going even in difficult times.
